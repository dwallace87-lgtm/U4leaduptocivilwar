Part C: Looking for Audience Clues

Read each line from the speech and answer the questions.

“I have as much muscle as any man, and can do as much work as any man.”

1a. What does this line tell you about what some people in the audience might think about women?

1b. Who is she trying to prove something to here: men, women, both, or someone else? Explain.

“As for intellect, all I can say is, if women have a pint and man a quart, why can’t she have her little pint full?”

2a. What is she saying about women’s minds and rights in this line?

2b. What kind of audience would understand this joke about “pint” and “quart”?

“I cant read, but I can hear. I have heard the bible and have learned that Eve caused man to sin.”

3a. What does this tell you about the audience’s beliefs or what they care about?

3b. Why do you think she brings up the Bible when she talks to this audience?

“And how came Jesus into the world? Through God who created him and woman who bore him. Man, where is your part?”

4a. Who is she speaking directly to in this line?

4b. What is she trying to make this audience think or feel?
